Hey! My name is Jeff.

I recently graduated from UCLA with a double major in Linguistics of Computer Science and Cognitive Science. Besides the fact that it makes me sound cool and smart, what I love about my combination of majors is that it represents what design is to me: a contemplation of what makes us human, and the constantly evolving technologies we use to express it.

How'd I get here?

If my major didn't give it away, I think that my secret sauce as a designer is my love of exploring new topics... which helps explain my background in product management, software development and orchestral performance. As a designer, my technical experience grants me a more holistic understanding of the product cycle, and even allows me to play a firsthand role in implementing the things I design (like this portfolio!) Meanwhile, my classical training as a violist has given me an appreciation for the precision that goes into making a sensory experience beautiful.

Why'd I stay?

I stumbled upon the world of design in college and instantly fell in love. I'd found a place where I could leverage my perfectionism—something I'd always considered a curse—to paint a beautiful bigger picture, while also tending to the small but impactful details that drive delightful experiences. As a longtime proponent of the philosophy of Marie Kondo, I believe that good design has the power to create a world that sparks more joy!

When I'm not designing things...

... you'll probably find me hosting a board game night, solving the daily NYT crossword, or learning any instrument I can get my hands on (I'm at 6 and counting!)
